**Across 40 schools in El Salvador, 250 students took their very first steps into a world that most adults never fully grasp.**
They earned their _Bitcoin Diploma_. They learned about the _Cypherpunk Manifesto_, about _privacy_, about why _sovereignty over your own money_ matters. They discovered how APIs work and how Bitcoin is more than a currency — it is a doorway to opportunity.

Only after this foundation was built did the next adventure begin. Last Wednesday marked the stunning finale of that adventure:  
The [LNBits](https://x.com/lnbits) Hackathon, organized by [NodeNation](https://x.com/NodeNationSV) and strongly supported by our interview partner [PurpusasG](https://x.com/PupusasG) and the whole community via [GeyserFund](https://geyser.fund/project/nodenation)
But calling it a hackathon doesn’t do it justice.
This is a mission.
A turning point.
A new chapter for young people who had never been invited into the world of building technology — now becoming active creators of it.

### The Start of Something Bigger

After earning their Bitcoin Diploma, the most motivated students continued into the next stage:  
A 7-week hackathon program, meeting once a week for four hours of coding, testing, breaking things, fixing things, learning — and, most importantly, discovering their own capability.  
From these students, 14 teams were formed, each made up of teenagers aged 14–16. Nine teachers guided them. The NodeNation team supported them.

The goal was simple: **learning by doing.**

And **LNbits** was the perfect platform for that.

### Why LNbits?

- Because LNbits is modular
- Because its API is open
- Because kids can build on it without running into closed walls
- Because it is fun

### What Happened Next Surprised Everyone

Organizers expected creative projects — but nothing prepared them for the ambition, structure, and imagination these students delivered.
They built tools no one expected.
They experimented, failed, tried again, and produced results that felt like a glimpse into the future.

If you want to explore their creations, every project was submitted through a GitHub-style page — documented and organized with the same professionalism you’d expect from junior developers starting their careers. [Full Project Docs](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1s1WWrpKojYddcGIfhVaR-sdSYlnkOKmrREzERTxGGBY/edit?gid=1783807453#gid=1783807453)

This week, at the EconomiaBitcoin Conference in Berlin (El Salvador), some of the students’ creations will be showcased with real users interacting with them for the first time.  
Some projects even integrate with the LNbits Bitcoin Switch Extension, activating in-game features or real-world actions — like turning on a bubble machine via a Lightning payment. [Twitter Post](https://x.com/DrShift3/status/1992779840785903903)
